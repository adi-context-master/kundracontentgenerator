I thought I was a model of good screen time. ğŸ˜‡
Until my 2.5-year-old daughter unwittingly put me in my place.

Two days ago, we were doing a puzzle together. Then I took my phone out of my pocket to reply to a message.

A completely harmless gesture... for me.

But not for her.

At just two and a half years old, she looked at me with a little pout and said, in a voice that was sad, serious, and insistent all at once:

"Mommy, phone in your pocket."

It left me speechless.

Me, who always thought I was a model of good screen time! And yet, it was she, little as she is, who reminded me what it means to "be present."

At that moment, I understood that children can also alert us, in their own way. They become our mirrors, true mediators, unwittingly revealing our own flaws.

It was a real wake-up call.

Now, if I decide to spend time with her, I want to give it my all. Not halfway.

And she senses it, believe me.
